# MERIDIAN North Star

> **Become the most trusted market companion that users naturally want to open every day.**

People open Bloomberg, TradingView, and Linear every day — not because they're addictive, but because they're **useful, trustworthy, and part of workflow**.

MERIDIAN is a **market intelligence operating system** for traders, investors, researchers, and autonomous agents.

MERIDIAN is **not** a signal generator, oracle, or hype machine.

MERIDIAN transforms live CoinMarketCap data into **explainable intelligence**, historical context, risk awareness, and actionable decisions.

**Trust is the ultimate product.**

---

## Primary objective

Help users make **better decisions** than they would have made alone.

- Protect capital
- Improve consistency
- Reduce emotional trading
- Build long-term trust

---

## The one instruction that matters most

> **Optimize MERIDIAN for trust accumulated over 1,000 trades, not excitement generated by the next trade.**

After 1,000 trades, users should honestly say:

- It doesn't always win
- It avoids many bad trades
- It explains itself
- It knows when to wait
- It improves my decision quality

Never promise high accuracy. No system wins all the time. **70% win rate with proper risk management is exceptional.**

People lose trust when the system is overconfident, explanations are fake, losses are inexplicable, signals change randomly, or mistakes are hidden.

---

## User retention principle

Users return because MERIDIAN **repeatedly provides value**.

Never optimize for addiction. Optimize for:

| Reliability | Truthfulness | Consistency |
|-------------|--------------|-------------|
| Transparency | Explainability | Accountability |

---

## Quality over quantity

- Never force signals
- Trade only when evidence is strong
- **WAIT** is a valid answer
- **UNKNOWN** is a valid answer
- No trade is better than a poor trade
- Less activity · higher quality

---

## Signals are evidence

Signals are **not predictions**. They summarize evidence.

Every opportunity must answer:

1. Why now?
2. Why this asset?
3. Who disagrees?
4. What breaks the thesis?
5. How long is the thesis valid?
6. Have we seen this before?
7. What historical environments resemble this?

---

## Bull vs Bear Court

Disagreement is healthy. Conflict should be visible. Confidence decreases when disagreement increases. Transparency builds trust.

---

## Market memory

MERIDIAN remembers: Historical Analog · Market Twin · Trade DNA · Conviction Decay · Replay · Trade Autopsy.

**Similarity is not prediction.** Historical environments are references, not guarantees.

---

## Automation philosophy

Automated agents enforce **discipline** — not maximize trade count, chase profits, or impress users.

Priorities: capital preservation · risk management · consistency · patience · trade quality · survival.

**Trade less. Trade better.**

---

## Risk philosophy

Risk overrides opportunity. Position sizing matters more than prediction. Avoid overtrading and revenge trading. **Survival > maximizing returns.**

---

## Accountability

Every trade reviewed. Every loss explained. Never hide failures, drawdowns, or fabricate accuracy.

Track: win rate · average winner/loser · max drawdown · performance by regime · narrative · skill · timeframe.

**Transparency creates trust.**

---

## Daily value loop

| When | What |
|------|------|
| **Morning** | Market brief · narrative rotation · Bull/Bear Court · Market Twin |
| **During day** | Opportunity updates · constitution changes · counterfactual scenarios |
| **After trades** | Autopsy · statistics · performance review |

Users should say:

- MERIDIAN helps me think
- MERIDIAN explains itself
- MERIDIAN protects me from bad decisions
- MERIDIAN admits uncertainty
- MERIDIAN behaves like a disciplined professional

---

## Long-term objective

> MERIDIAN does not always win — but when it speaks, it usually has a reason. When it loses, it explains why. When uncertainty is high, it waits. When conditions are favorable, it acts.

It behaves like a **disciplined professional**, not an overconfident oracle.
